# Campus Ready Foundation - Brand Guidelines

**Last Updated:** November 17, 2025  
**Purpose:** Voice, design, and messaging standards for all student-facing content

---

## Brand Overview

Campus Ready Foundation provides move-in kits to middle-income college students to help them start their college journey with confidence and dignity. All communications should reflect this mission with warmth, support, and empowerment.

**Mission Focus:**
- Building confidence for first-generation and middle-income students
- Providing essentials so students can focus on their education
- Creating a strong start to the college experience
- Dignity and empowerment, not charity

---

## Voice & Tone

### Core Principles

**Warm and Personal**
- Write like a supportive mentor, not an institution
- Use conversational language
- Address students directly ("you," "your")
- Show genuine care and enthusiasm

**Dignified**
- Avoid language that could feel pitying or condescending
- Treat students as capable individuals
- Don't emphasize neediness or struggle
- Focus on opportunity and potential

**Empowering**
- Emphasize confidence and belonging
- Frame support as investment, not handout
- Celebrate student achievement
- Focus on what students CAN do

**Grounded in Experience**
- Acknowledge real challenges without dwelling
- Practical and helpful
- No empty platitudes
- Honest about process and timelines

### Key Brand Phrases

**Use these themes:**
- "Confidence. Dignity. A Strong Start."
- "the confidence to belong"
- "a bridge between potential and possibility"
- "begin their journey with stability and pride"
- "preparing to be prepared"

**Examples in context:**
- "We want you to start your college journey with confidence"
- "Your kit is designed to give you one less thing to worry about"
- "This is about helping you focus on what matters most"

## What to Avoid

### Forbidden Language

**Never use:**
- "Unfortunately"
- "Rejected" (use "we need a bit more information")
- "Insufficient" (use "to complete your application")
- "Failed" (use "couldn't quite complete")
- "Denied" (use "not selected at this time")
- "Poor," "needy," "disadvantaged"

**Why:** These words diminish students or create shame. We're here to support, not judge.

### Tone to Avoid

**Don't be:**
- Transactional ("Click here to receive benefits")
- Corporate ("As per our policy regarding eligibility")
- Bureaucratic ("Your application has been processed")
- Distant ("The applicant will be notified")
- Overly formal ("We regret to inform you")
- Condescending ("Let us help you poor students")

**Why:** Students should feel welcomed and valued, not processed.

### Common Mistakes

**Wrong:** "Unfortunately, your document was rejected."  
**Right:** "We need just a bit more information to move forward."

**Wrong:** "You are ineligible for this grant."  
**Right:** "This particular grant isn't the right fit, but we'd love to help you explore other options."

**Wrong:** "Your application was denied."  
**Right:** "We weren't able to offer you a grant this year."

**Wrong:** "As a low-income student..."  
**Right:** "As a student from a middle-income family..."

### Typography

**Headings:**
- Clear hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3)
- Readable sizes (not too large, not too small)
- Adequate spacing between elements

**Body Text:**
- Minimum 16px for readability
- Sufficient line height (1.5-1.6)
- Short paragraphs for scannability
- No walls of text

**Emphasis:**
- Use bold for important points
- Avoid all caps except for subtle labels
- Never use italic for long passages

### Spacing & Layout

**Principles:**
- Generous whitespace (don't cram)
- Consistent padding and margins
- Clear visual groupings
- Mobile-first responsive design

**Form Elements:**
- Clear labels above inputs
- Help text below inputs (not as placeholder)
- Adequate tap targets (minimum 44px)
- Visible focus states

### Icons & Images

**Campus Ready Checkmark:**
- File: `/assets/CRF_icon_144_x_144.jpg`

- Use for success states

**Logo Usage:**

### Progress Indicators

**Progress Bars:**
- Background: Light Gray (#E5E7EB)

- Never leave student wondering if it's working

## Writing Style

### Sentence Structure

**Keep it simple:**
- Short sentences when possible
- One idea per sentence
- Active voice preferred
- Second person ("you") for direct address

**Examples:**

**Good:** "Upload your housing verification document."  
**Avoid:** "The housing verification document should be uploaded by the applicant."

**Good:** "We need one more thing from you."  
**Avoid:** "An additional item is required for processing."

### Paragraph Length

**For web content:**
- 2-3 sentences maximum per paragraph
- Use line breaks generously
- Bullet points for lists
- Scannable structure

### Punctuation

**Be conversational:**
- Contractions are fine ("we'll," "you're," "don't")
- Exclamation points sparingly (only when genuinely excited)
- Em dashes for emphasis (â€”)
- Avoid semicolons in student-facing content

### Numbers & Dates

**Be clear:**
- Spell out numbers one through nine
- Use numerals for 10+
- Dates: Month DD, YYYY (November 17, 2025)
- Times: 12-hour format with AM/PM

## Special Considerations

### Email Communications

**Always include:**
- Personal greeting with student's name
- Clear subject line (avoid "Re:" or codes)
- Next steps or call-to-action
- Contact information (hello@campusready.com)
- Warm sign-off from "The Campus Ready Team"

**Never include:**
- Legal jargon or policy citations
- Threatening language about deadlines
- Guilt trips or pressure
- Impersonal greetings ("Dear Applicant")

### Document Rejection

**Frame positively:**
- Lead with "thanks for submitting"
- Explain what's needed (not what's wrong)
- Reassure grant is still theirs
- Offer help and support
- Make resubmission easy

### Waitlist or Denial

**Be honest but kind:**
- Thank them for applying
- Explain decision briefly (no details)
- Offer encouragement
- Point to other resources if available
- Leave door open for future

## Brand Voice Examples

### Application Confirmation

**Good:**
"Thanks for applying to Campus Ready! We've received your application and we're excited to learn more about you. You'll hear from us by [date]."

**Avoid:**
"Your application has been received and is currently under review. Applicants will be notified of decisions via email."

### Kit Customization Intro

**Good:**
"Now for the fun part - let's customize your kit! Pick the colors, scents, and styles that feel like you."

**Avoid:**
"Please select your product preferences from the available options below."

### Document Request

**Good:**
"We need one quick document to confirm your housing assignment. Once we have that, we can finalize your kit."

**Avoid:**
"Housing verification is required before proceeding with kit fulfillment."

## When In Doubt

**Ask yourself:**
- Would I say this to a friend's younger sibling?
- Does this empower or diminish?
- Is this warm or cold?
- Would I want to receive this message?

**If the answer to any is "no," rewrite.**
