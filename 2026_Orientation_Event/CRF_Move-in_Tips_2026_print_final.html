A Note from Campus Ready

We put together these move-in tips to set you up for success. Read through the list before you leave. Also visit your school's residence life page. Most importantly, planning now saves time and stress later.

Before You Leave
Prepare to be Prepared
01
Set and read your school email
Set it up before you leave. Your school will send you move-in details, your check-in window, and room assignment.
02
Call your roommate
Reach out before move-in day. Introduce yourself. It's a lot easier to walk into a new room when you already know who's on the other side of it.
03
Gather your documents
Gov't ID, health insurance card, financial aid letter. Physical copies + a photo of each on your phone. At 18 your medical records transfer to you. To keep your family in the loop, sign a HIPAA release before you leave.
04
If you're flying, ship, don't check your boxes
Fly with one week of essentials in your luggage. The rest can be waiting for you when you arrive. Your Lyft credit from us will get you from the airport to campus.
05
Research your arrival before you leave home
Find your check-in location (it may not be your dorm), the unloading zone, where carts are, and how to activate your meal plan. Twenty minutes now saves hours on move-in day.
Move-In Day
Start Strong
06
Know how you're getting there
Driving: use the unloading zone. Lyft: have the driver stop there. Traveling alone: ask a move-in volunteer or RA to help. That's what they're there for.
07
Do the room condition report carefully
Document any damage in your room: walls, carpet, fixtures. Photos are your evidence. Anything undocumented may be charged at move-out.
08
Choose your bed. Then make it.
Once chosen, make the bed before anything else. A made bed makes the room feel like yours.
09
After your room is set up
Meet your RA. Put their number in your phone. Attend Orientation!
First 48 Hours
Stay Strong
10
Be kind to yourself
Get your School ID. Find the cafeteria and activate your meal plan. Find these places: gym, health center, financial aid office, library, your first classroom. Say yes to one thing you didn't plan to do. Above all else remember: What you feel in the first weeks is normal. You belong right where you are and you'll get more comfortable every day.
